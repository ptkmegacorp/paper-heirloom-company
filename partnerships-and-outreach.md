# Paper Heirloom Company — Partnerships & Outreach

**Status:** Draft  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-03  
**Goal:** Reach ~100 contacts to start — micro-influencers, planners, complementary sellers, bloggers, FB community

---

## Best partnership types (ranked for early stage)

### 1. Micro-influencers (best ROI early)

**Who:** 1k–25k followers — bridal / wedding planning / DIY / “soft life” aesthetic. Not mega influencers.

**Where:** Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, small YouTube (“planning my wedding” vlogs).

**Offer:**

- Free set (50-guest tier) for honest content
- Custom code (`SARAH10`) + **15–20% commission** on sales (track via Etsy coupon or unique link)
- Or flat fee $50–150 for 1 Reel + 3 Stories if engagement is solid

**Why:** Cheap, visual product, strong story (“fold at shower → use at wedding”). One good Reel beats 10 passive affiliates.

---

### 2. Complementary Etsy sellers (cross-promo)

**Who:** Non-competing sellers — calligraphy place cards, bridal shower games, wedding favors, invitation designers, bridal robes, cake toppers.

**Offer:**

- Bundle mention in thank-you inserts
- Shared Instagram Story swap
- “Shop our friend” in listing description
- Optional: 10% referral on custom orders

**Why:** Same buyer, zero ad cost, Etsy traffic patterns favor internal discovery.

---

### 3. Wedding planners & day-of coordinators

**Who:** Local + virtual planners on Instagram; those selling “experience” not just logistics.

**Offer:**

- **Wholesale** (30–40% off) for client gifts
- **Referral fee** ($25–75 per booking) or free sample kit
- One-pager PDF for clients: “unique shower activity that becomes reception décor”

**Why:** One planner = multiple weddings/year; differentiated client gifts stand out.

---

### 4. Bridal bloggers & newsletter writers (small)

**Who:** Independent sites, Substack bride newsletters, Pinterest-heavy blogs (5k–50k monthly visits) — not major magazines.

**Offer:** Free product + affiliate commission + ready-made photos/copy (hero infographic works well).

**Find them:** Google `"bridal shower gift ideas" + blog`; Pinterest pins linking to small blogs.

---

### 5. Affiliate platforms (secondary — harder at small scale)

| Platform | Fit |
|----------|-----|
| **Etsy Affiliate** (Rakuten) | Promoters earn on Etsy broadly — you don’t control who picks your listing |
| **ShareASale / Awin** | Only if you have own site + checkout |
| **LTK / ShopMy** | Works if influencers already use it; wedding is a smaller slice |
| **Amazon Associates** | Not relevant if Etsy-only |

**Reality:** At launch, **direct deals with 50 micro-partners** beat joining a big network. Track manually: name, code, commission, paid date.

## Facebook groups

**Good for:** Research, relationships, soft mentions.  
**Bad for:** Dropping Etsy links (admins ban quickly).

**Search terms:**

- “Bridal shower ideas”
- “Wedding planning [year]”
- “Budget wedding” / “DIY wedding”
- “Engaged / bride tribe [region]”
- “Wedding vendor recommendations [city]”

**Approach:**

1. Join 10–15 groups; **lurk ~2 weeks**, comment helpfully on “shower activity ideas?” threads
2. When asked for interactive gifts, share the **concept** first — not the link
3. Link only if rules allow or when someone DMs
4. Message **group admins** separately: free set for pinned “vendor spotlight” if allowed

**Target from FB:** ~20 warm contacts (active members, admins, frequent posters) — not 100 cold links.

Only count someone as **qualified** before you spend real effort (custom pitch, free kit, wholesale sheet). See **Qualified contact (BANT)** below.

## Qualified contact (BANT)

A **qualified contact** is someone worth a personalized outreach — not just a name on a list. They must pass all four:

| Letter | Question | Qualified = yes when… |
|--------|----------|------------------------|
| **B — Budget** | Can they afford the fit (money, time, or trade)? | The offer matches what they can spend or accept: gifted set, 15% code, wholesale tier, or cross-promo swap — without stretching your margin or theirs. |
| **A — Authority** | Can they actually say yes? | They control the decision: shop owner, creator on their own channel, planner who picks client gifts, blogger with editorial say, or FB admin / trusted voice — not an assistant or “maybe my friend knows someone.” |
| **N — Need** | Is there a real fit for our product? | Their audience or clients care about **bridal shower gifts**, **interactive shower activities**, or **wedding table / place card** moments — not generic wedding décor with no shower angle. |
| **T — Time** | Is now a good time? | There’s a near-term reason to act: wedding/shower season, active content calendar, clients with showers in the next **3–9 months**, or they’re visibly planning/posting about showers *now* — not “someday maybe.” |

**Rule:** **B + A + N + T all yes → qualified.** Any clear **no** → deprioritize or nurture later.

### Signals by bucket

| Bucket | Budget | Authority | Need | Time |
|--------|--------|-----------|------|------|
| **Micro-influencer** | Will post for gift or accepts affiliate terms; not expecting $500+ upfront | Owns IG/TikTok/Pinterest; posts bridal/DIY/wedding content | Recent posts on shower gifts, wedding planning, or DIY table details | Posting weekly+; engaged audience; bridal season or “engaged” content in last 90 days |
| **Planner / coordinator** | Buys client gifts or refers vendors; wholesale or referral fee works | Names vendors in client materials; replies as the business | Clients want experiential showers or custom table moments | Has weddings/shower clients on calendar this season |
| **Etsy seller (complementary)** | Cross-promo is enough; no cash deal required | Shop owner or co-owner | Sells adjacent products (games, favors, calligraphy, robes) — **not** competing origami place cards | Active shop; recent sales/reviews; listing updates in last 60 days |
| **Blogger / newsletter** | Gift + affiliate OK; no paid placement required at launch | Writes or approves gift-guide / shower content | Site/newsletter covers shower gifts or wedding planning | Published shower/wedding gift content in last 12 months or issue planned this season |
| **FB power user / admin** | Relationship cost only; no product until rapport | Respected voice or admin in bridal/wedding group | Group threads ask for shower activities and gift ideas | Active in group; you’ve lurked/commented; rules allow soft mentions |

### Disqualifiers (any one = not qualified)

- Competing origami place card or shower-activity seller  
- No bridal/wedding/shower overlap in content or clients  
- Bot account, inactive >6 months, or bought followers  
- Can’t accept gift ship (non-US if you only ship domestically, etc.)  
- “Love it but no weddings until next year” with no content reason → **nurture**, not active pipeline  

### Tracker use

In your spreadsheet, add columns **B / A / N / T** (Y/N) and **Qualified** (auto: all four Y).  
**Outreach order:** qualified first → sent → replied → yes → posted → paid.

**Target mix:** Of your ~100 names, aim for **~40–50 qualified** before heavy sending; the rest stay on a nurture list.

>

>
> No pressure on timeline — only if it feels like a fit for your community.
>
> [Link to hero infographic or Etsy when live]
>
> Thanks,
> [Name]

Personalize one line about their last post.

## Deal structures (pick 2–3 max)

1. **Gifted product** — lowest friction; ask for honest review + 3 photos
2. **Code + 15–20% commission** — professional; track in spreadsheet
3. **Wholesale for planners** — ~40% off list; they mark up or bundle into packages
4. **Giveaway collab** — good for engagement, weaker for direct sales

Avoid heavy discounting (>25%) early — undercuts “~10% above Etsy comps” positioning (see `pricing.md`).

## Where to find 100 names

- **Instagram hashtags:** `#bridalshowerideas` `#weddingplanning` `#engaged2026` `#diywedding` — save accounts with 1k–20k followers
- **TikTok:** “bridal shower gift ideas,” “wedding planning tips” — filter small creators
- **Etsy:** “bridal shower gift,” “wedding place cards” — message complementary shops
- **Google:** `"wedding planner" + city` + Instagram
- **Podcasts:** small wedding podcasts (10–500 reviews) — guest or sponsor swap
- **Pinterest:** accounts pinning shower ideas with consistent boards

**Tools (optional):** Spreadsheet, Notion, Apollo (planner emails), Hunter.io (blogger emails).

## Deprioritize for now

- Big affiliate networks (low conversion until you have traffic)
- The Knot / WeddingWire paid ads (expensive, competitive)
- Mass cold DMs with Etsy links in FB groups (bans + bad brand)
- Mega influencers (won’t reply; wrong price point)
